Why I Like Running

Running is one of those things I complain about while doing it and then immediately miss once I stop. This page is a small tribute to sweating, questionable life choices, and endorphins.

Why I Like Running

Here are the very serious and highly scientific reasons why running has my heart:

  • It’s socially acceptable to wear big-ass glasses and all kinds of tech wearables at the same time.
  • It lets me buy expensive running shoes without guilt (this is called self-care).
  • Problems feel smaller when I’m too tired to think about them.
  • The post-run shower feels like a luxury spa experience.

How I Got Into Running

Running wasn’t love at first step. It was more like a slow, awkward friendship.

1. I started running to “do something different.” 3. I survived my first 10-minute run. 4. I accidentally signed up for a race. 5. Stockholm syndrome kicked in. 6. Now I run voluntarily.

Favorite Places to Run

Some places just make running better (or at least more tolerable):

  • Parks with trees and shade
  • Anywhere with a good view that distracts me from my legs

Final Thoughts

Running isn’t always easy, fun, or graceful — but that’s kind of the point. It’s just me, the road, my thoughts, and the promise of snacks afterward.

Now excuse me while I stretch and dramatically complain about my calves.
